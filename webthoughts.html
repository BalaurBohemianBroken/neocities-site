why did i start a website here?

i guess mostly i've been lonely. there was one person i used to talk to about all of my techy projects , but they're not around anymore and i'm trying to move on. i'm sort of hoping that i can meet people who understand something about me in a way most don't.
i need to talk about the things on my mind. i used to do it at my friends, but there's more than any one person should be subjected to. i journal often, but i wanna revisit some of my thoughts, catalogue them, develop them, and let people engage with them on their own terms. so, website

i've never been able to do social media. it feels too distant, uncontrolled, diluted, it just makes me feel even more alone. but something like this, people can do anything they want. that's beautiful, it can say so much.

i also need a project, all the time. in the past that was game dev, maybe again sometime. of writing, i'm finishing college, and that's kept me busy for a bit, but not really. i do a lot of small things, like hiding hyperlinks everywhere all the time.

i've sort of had a website in the past, but it was run locally and i almost never shared any of my projects on it with others. i wanna work towards porting some of them over though.

sites i like
these may sit on this page, or i may move them somewhere unique. i have a few ideas that could do with the space.
The Possum Den: this cool critter runs a radio! they have custom made parody ads, interviews occasionally. as of writing they're just getting back into doing more live events after a hiatus. we've spoken a little, and they just seem neat.

stupied stuff!: i just like this site. it's well made and easy to explore, but has so much to explore. it's such a detailed capture of the creator.

Internet Archive: you surely know this place. they are flawed, but they are also one of few places that have altruistic goals and the means to execute on them. i have their wayback plugin and contribute to the archive; if you explore the internet, you should consider preserving it. also, check out their gifcities page if you want to find some vintage stuff.

https://www.tooooools.app/effects/distort: this site has a few very cool visual effects, i can't deny that. i'm unhappy that the author seems to have an AI focus outside of this, and i really don't trust the longevity of the page. but it is cool, and i do find it useful.

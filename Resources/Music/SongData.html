Andrew Bird

Imitosis

I got shown this song by someone back when I was ~14, in a relationship I really shouldn't have been in.
It's the second oldest song that connects me to my youth that I still listen to, the first being Woodkid's Iron. They both hold an important role for me in remembering what I went through.
Andrew Bird stands out among the artists I listen to. As of writing, I haven't quite found anyone who does what he does, how he does it.

Baltyk

The Bus Home (I Hope You're Alright)

I've listened to this song exclusively for days at a time. It has forged itself into the core of 2025 and 2026.
It's amateur, it has some rough edges. The vocal volume shifts after the first chorus. I think that is what I want in this song.
I found this song by slim chance, and precisely the right time. The way the vocals in the final chorus overlap, conflict and complement. The specific imagery of sitting at the back of the bus, thinking about someone you don't speak to anymore, wondering if they got better, because you haven't. Sending a wall of text at someone out of nowhere.
It had felt that if someone understood this song, they might understand how I've felt and who I've been, though that did not end up being the case. At least, someone who reads this might know it.

As I listen to this song and write this, I think I might finally be leaving the place it found me in. But I could never forget it.

Baltyk

Self-help (Panic)

This song connects to a small but intense point in my life. I had survived my worst year by a great margin, and while still very broken, I was beginning to become stable.
It was not just my life, but the lives of many of my friends, too. At that time I lived entirely with the goal of supporting those I cared for. It was what cemented to me that studying to be a therapist might be the right call - though the profession is very distinct from friendship.
Towards the end of the song, they share some of their mantras: 'It is not my job to fix others.' 'It is my job to make me happy.' 'I have a right to my own feelings.' 'I am enough.'
The chorus loops, 'Some people cannot be helped, and you matter! I have changed for the better.'
These spoke directly to me at a time when I was learning to rely on myself. I adopted them fully, I still use them, and added some of my own.

The song speaks of intense emotions and experiences, the instrumentation is solemn, but it remains calm. That juxtaposition has helped me express my emotions while quelling my fear many times.

Skye was a huge inspiration for me at the end of 2025. I'll probably talk about that period of time more on the song that drove me.

Their music is set in a post-apocalyptic world, following three characters. Izi, Enth, and Ila. This song focuses on Enth. Or rather, what Enth is running from.
Enth is followed, or haunted, or possessed by something. At this point we haven't seen much of it, but we know it's dangerous, and we know Enth fears it. They regularly flee from their friends out of fear that they might hurt them.
The final words of the album are, I believe Izi speaking to Enth, trying to convince them to stay. Enth ends the album by saying, "I don't understand. It's like you wanna die."

At least, that's my interpretation. This song gives us an insight into what Enth is running from. The tracklist shows us which character each song is from the perspective of: this one is censored, or static. It isn't one of the main 3.
The opening of the song is calm, a little jazzy, almost cocky as it taunts its target with its inevitability. I'll awake / With your eyes / Lost in the fire / Pray for god here but I remain
As it progresses, a distorted guitar takes over, and the vocals turn to wordless whines. It builds towards a frenetic crescendo as though the music itself is in flight from its predator, and finalizes slow and defeated.
The character of Enth channels a lot of negative emotions. Depression most clearly. Insecurity. This is a song that shows a different side, a fear of their self.
I don't know many songs that capture that blend of character, and this song in particular... The imagery of being taunted by this thing that feels like it has absolute control over you, that it might at any moment shred you and everything you love, that maybe the only way to save everyone from that is remove anything for it to destroy. That's powerful.

As of writing I've been working on a poem inspired by this song, one of hundreds that probably won't see the light of day.
